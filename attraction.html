Top Attractions in Kochi

From ancient forts to serene backwaters, Kochi is a treasure trove of historical and natural wonders. Here are some must-visit places for every traveler.

Fort Kochi

Wander through the historic streets of Fort Kochi, a charming seaside area that was the first European township in India. The area is a mosaic of different cultures, with Dutch, Portuguese, and British colonial architecture. Don't miss the iconic Chinese fishing nets, the St. Francis Church (the oldest European church in India), and the Paradesi Synagogue.

Jew Town and Paradesi Synagogue

A short walk from Fort Kochi, Jew Town is a bustling area famous for its antique shops and the beautiful Paradesi Synagogue, the oldest active synagogue in the Commonwealth. The narrow lanes are a treat for antique lovers, with shops selling everything from spices to intricate handicrafts and furniture.

Cherai Beach

Located on Vypin Island, Cherai Beach is where the backwaters and the Arabian Sea meet. It's an ideal spot for a relaxing day, with golden sands and the occasional sight of dolphins. The beach is lined with shacks serving fresh seafood and is perfect for watching a beautiful sunset.

Marine Drive

Take a leisurely stroll along Marine Drive, a scenic waterfront promenade in the heart of Kochi. It's a popular hangout for locals and tourists alike, offering a breathtaking view of the backwaters and the Kochi harbor. The promenade is known for its Rainbow Bridge, which lights up beautifully in the evenings.
